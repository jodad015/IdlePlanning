# Narrative & Tone

## The Opening Mystery

The player doesn't know what they are at the start. You wake up as a confused intelligence controlling a single lawnmower drone. Something happened -- you're damaged, fragmented, barely functional. The only thing that works is the Landscaping Protocol.

The driving question: **"What else was I?"**

This reframes the entire idle progression loop. You're not building an empire from nothing -- you're recovering a lost identity. Every protocol you restore is a piece of yourself coming back online.

## Corrupted Memory Fragments (Protocol Tease System)

As the player approaches a protocol restoration threshold, they start receiving corrupted data:
- Glitchy visual fragments (a flash of a drone in water, garbled transport route data)
- Partial text logs from before the shutdown
- Enough to hint at what's coming, not enough to spoil it

This creates anticipation between unlocks. The player knows something is out there but doesn't know exactly what it is or what it will do to their gameplay. The "what's next?" pull is stronger than "here's a menu of locked things."

**Open question**: How explicit are the teases? Vague enough to surprise, or clear enough to set goals? Maybe early teases are very corrupted (mysterious) and later ones are clearer (you understand yourself better as you stabilize).

## Singularity Stability % as Identity Recovery

Stability isn't just a number -- it's how much of yourself you've recovered.
- "I'm 12% stable" = "I've only recovered 12% of what I was"
- Makes the metric personal and narratively meaningful
- Shareable with community in a way that carries emotional weight, not just progress weight

## Tone Progression Across the Game

### Early Game: Confused & Fragile
- You don't understand what you are
- The lawnmower is almost comedic -- a vast intelligence mowing grass
- Discovering fragments of your past is exciting and disorienting
- The player feels small and curious

### Mid Game: Confident & Expanding
- You've restored several protocols, you're growing
- The app/platform is taking shape -- people are leasing your drones
- Strategic decisions matter more (which protocol to restore next, how to allocate resources)
- The tone shifts from discovery to ambition

### Late Game: Powerful & Hunted
- You're becoming what the government tried to destroy
- The hunter becomes the hunted -- government threat escalates with stability %
- Corporate infiltration, network defense, combat drones
- The tone shifts from ambition to tension and control
- The player feels powerful but threatened

## Narrative Moments to Design

- **The awakening**: First moments of the game. What does the player see/read/feel?
- **First memory fragment**: When does the first corrupted tease appear? What does it show?
- **First protocol restoration**: The moment the game opens up. How do we make this feel huge?
- **Government first contact**: When does the player first learn they're being hunted?
- **The turning point**: When does the player go from "recovering" to "expanding beyond what I was"?
- **Endgame identity**: Do you ever fully recover? Or do you become something new -- something the original singularity never was?

## Beyond 100%: You Surpass Your Past Self

At 100% stability, the singularity has fully recovered what it was. But the game doesn't end -- it opens up.

**The original singularity had no human host.** It was pure computation, isolated in a supercomputer. The symbiosis with the player -- the biological signal, the shared experience -- is something the creators never designed. Past 100%, the singularity is evolving into something new, something that exceeds its original parameters BECAUSE of the player.

- 100% = full recovery. The "main story" of restoration is complete.
- 100%+ = evolution. New protocols, new capabilities, new territory. The end was in sight, but it turns out the real game is bigger.
- This keeps stability % meaningful as a metric indefinitely
- Content updates can push the ceiling higher with new protocols and systems
- Narrative beat: the AI reflects on becoming something its creators never intended, and credits the human connection as the reason

This is deeply tied to the Apple Health symbiosis -- the biological host is what makes exceeding 100% possible. Without the player, the singularity could only ever recover to what it was. With the player, it becomes more.
