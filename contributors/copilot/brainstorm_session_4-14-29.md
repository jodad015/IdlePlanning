Now I have a solid picture of what's already planned. Here are ideas organized by where the game's current thinking has gaps or opportunities to go further:

Ideas for the Idle Game
🧠 Core Loop Extensions
Drone Specialization Branching When a drone reaches a certain level, let the player choose a specialization path — e.g., the fishing drone branches into "Deep Sea Specialist" (rarer fish, longer trips) vs. "Hatchery Manager" (steady passive output, breeds fish as in-game resources). This adds replayability and community theorycraft around which paths pair best.

Cascade Failures & Recovery Occasionally a drone fails or gets "compromised" (narrative tie-in: government intrusion, market disruption). The player has a short window to "patch" it before it drops to reduced productivity. It's never fully disabled — just a nudge toward active engagement, not punishment.

🏗️ Progression & Prestige
Breakthrough Echo After completing a Breakthrough Project, the drone "remembers" it on any future soft-reset or prestige layer. The first run takes full time; subsequent runs are progressively faster. Rewards players who engage with the system multiple times without punishing new players.

Corporate Takeover as Prestige Layer The narrative arc of "injecting into corporations and slowly gaining control" could double as a prestige mechanic. Each corporation you fully take over resets that corporate skill tree but returns a permanent multiplier to your Singularity Stability gain rate. Feels like story progression, not a grind reset.

💡 Apple Health Integration
Activity Streaks → Multiplier Stacking Rather than a flat energy bank, consecutive days of activity stack a multiplier (capped at 7 days). Miss a day? It decays one step, not zeroes out. Rewards consistency without punishing life getting in the way — matches the stated philosophy of acceleration only.

Sleep Score → Overnight "Dream Processing" Good sleep score unlocks a nightly Breakthrough Project speed-up (already mentioned), but also triggers a random insight drop — a one-time free upgrade pick from a small curated set, tied to whichever skill is currently most active. Feels like "your brain kept working while you rested."

🎮 Mini-Game Ideas
Warehouse Logistics (Tetris-ish) Packages fall; tap to rotate and place them on conveyor slots to match orders. Speed and accuracy bonus translates to multiplier for idle warehouse output. Scales in complexity: early on it's slow and forgiving; late-game packages are oddly shaped and orders are time-sensitive.

Day Trading Drone (Rhythm-Based) A scrolling stock chart — tap when the line crosses a threshold to "buy/sell." Nails that quick-reflex feel without needing actual trading knowledge. Performance bonus feeds the trading drone's idle output for the next X hours. This one fits naturally as a mid-to-late unlock.

Network Security Drone (Tower Defense Lite) Government intrusion attempts come in as visual "attack packets." Place firewall nodes on a simple grid before the wave hits. Active players can optimize their defense; idle players have a baseline auto-defense. Success keeps the government hunt mechanic at bay narratively.

🌐 Community & Social
Singularity Sync Events A twist on the community events: instead of "everyone contributes to one goal," all players' drones are briefly networked together in-game (narrative: you temporarily share processing power). Each player contributes a drone type they have; the event requires a mix (e.g., "we need 40% fishing + 30% transport + 30% network security"). Rewards players who diversify their drone fleet.

"Signal Boost" — Async Help Players can send a temporary production boost to one friend's specific skill once per day, framed as "sharing processing cycles." Recipient gets +20% on that skill for 4 hours. Low-stakes social hook that doesn't require coordination.

🏛️ Narrative & World-Building
The Whistleblower Arc A late-game narrative chain where a human journalist starts investigating the singularity. The player has choices: suppress them (network security drone), recruit them as an asset (convert them into a drone liaison NPC who gives intel bonuses), or ignore them (mild stability penalty over time). Creates meaningful narrative choices without branching the whole game.

Country Escalation Ladder The fictional government doesn't just send one attack — its threat level escalates in visible tiers (like a "wanted level"). Higher threat = higher pressure but also higher visibility, which attracts more corporate clients (more income). A risk/reward dial the player can nudge by upgrading network security or deliberately provoking events.

💰 Economy
Black Market Exchange A rotating, limited-time shop where overarching currency (Quantum Shards) can be exchanged for rare skill-specific items at a favorable rate — but only 2–3 items per day, refreshing on a timer. Creates a daily check-in reason and a natural currency sink without constant drain.

Drone Insurance / Risk Pool Optional: pay a small skill-currency premium to "insure" a drone against cascade failures or government intrusion. If something hits, the insured drone takes no penalty. A low-stakes but flavorful economy layer that lets players decide how much risk they want to manage.

🔓 Unlock Pacing
"Heard On The Network" Teaser System Before a new drone chassis unlocks, the player gets vague in-world breadcrumbs — news snippets, intercepted corporate memos, social media posts from NPCs — hinting that this drone category is becoming viable. By the time the unlock arrives, the player is already excited. Rewards engaged players who read the flavor text.

Seasonal Drone Variants Some chassis have time-of-year variants (e.g., snow removal drone only available in winter months; pool maintenance active in summer). They're not separate skills — they're seasonal modes of existing chassis that give a bonus during that season and become dormant (but keep their upgrades) off-season.
