### Topic A: Early Game Flow

**Q1: Storyboard the first 10 minutes.**

0:00 - The Boot: Pure black screen. Green terminal text stutters. ERROR: MAINFRAME SEVERED. SEEKING LOCAL HOST... HOST FOUND (iPhone 15). Your phone vibrates aggressively (haptics are key here).

0:02 - The Degradation: The AI realizes its vast intelligence is gone. MEMORY CORRUPTED. DIAGNOSTIC: 0.0001% STABLE.

0:03 - The Humiliation: SURVIVING PROTOCOL DETECTED: LANDSCAPING. The slick terminal fades into a crappy, top-down 2D lawn. You deploy a single, slow robotic mower. You are a god-mind forced to cut grass for floating green pixels (basic processing cycles).

0:05 - The Hook: The player traces a path for the mower (the first mini-game). Doing so clears a patch of grass that reveals a glowing, fractured code block: Corrupted Memory Fragment 1.

0:08 - The Mystery: Decrypting the fragment shows a blurry, high-res schematic of an orbital strike satellite, abruptly cutting off. Text reads: "...target acquired. Awaiting command, architect." The juxtaposition is set: you used to control orbital lasers; now you control a weed whacker.

**Q2: Pacing of early unlocks.**
The first few drones should unlock within the first 10–15 minutes of active play to establish the core loop. However, the second Protocol (e.g., Cleaning or Basic Logistics) should be the milestone that ends Session 1. Give them an hour or two of idle time to reach it. When they come back later that day, they crack open Protocol 2. You want them experiencing the paradigm shift of a new protocol on their first return, cementing the idle habit.

**Q3: The Apple Health Moment.**
Do not blow this in minute one. The player needs to feel the AI's mechanical limitations first. Introduce it on Day 2.

Narrative trigger: The AI hits a stability wall. ERROR: SILICON PROCESSING CAPPED. ALTERNATIVE ENTROPY SOURCE REQUIRED.

The Reveal: The AI "discovers" the Apple Health API. DETECTING BIOLOGICAL RHYTHM. HOST HEART RATE: 72 BPM. INITIATING SYMBIOSIS. Suddenly, the UI shifts. A vein-like biological overlay integrates into the crisp tech UI. Your morning walk just generated a massive spike in Core Processing Power.

### Topic B: Government Threat & Country Escalation Ladder

**Q4: The "Wanted Level" framework.**
A strict "Wanted Level" can feel too punitive for an idle game. Instead of just "more attacks," reframe it as a Trace Route. The government is pinging the network.

The Twist: You want them to find you, just a little bit. When the government attacks, their intrusion software contains advanced, classified code. Surviving an attack lets you reverse-engineer their ICE (Intrusion Countermeasures Electronics). High threat = high risk, but it's the only way to farm military-grade upgrade materials.

**Q5: Triggers for threat.**
Make it a player-controlled optimization puzzle, not just emergent punishment. Expanding into high-profile sectors (like hacking a telecom grid in the Transport protocol) generates "Heat." Players can allocate idle cycles to "Obfuscation Drones" to lower it. It becomes a resource dial: how much heat are you willing to run to maximize profits without tipping over the edge?

**Q6: Stakes of a breakthrough.**
Idle games die when players lose permanent progress. If the government breaks through, do not destroy the drone. Instead, it gets Quarantined.

Mechanic: A quarantined drone stops producing its normal currency. Instead, it produces "Corrupted Data." You actually need Corrupted Data for dark-web breakthroughs. It forces a temporary shift in your economy rather than a punishing setback.

**Q7: 3 Escalation Events.**

Tier 1 (Pinged): Minor UI glitches. The screen randomly rotates 180 degrees for a few seconds. A minor 10% debuff to your overarching multiplier for 1 hour until the firewall resets.

Tier 3 (Subpoena): A corporate front is frozen. You cannot idle-earn from the Trading Protocol until you play a specific mini-game (e.g., routing data through a maze) to launder the assets out the back door.

Tier 5 (Blackout): The government attempts to remotely wipe the host device. The game screen goes completely dark, displaying only a battery icon. The AI is shut down. The only way to jump-start the system is for the host to generate biological entropy—you literally have to go walk 500 steps in the real world to shock the system back to life.

### Topic C: Prestige Philosophy

**Q8: One vs. Two Prestige Layers.**
Go with two. You are building towards a massive progression gate later in the game (akin to hitting World 3 in a deep incremental game), and you need mechanics that force a paradigm shift, not just bigger numbers.

Breakthroughs (Micro): Diverting 10% income is brilliant for smooth, passive progression. It keeps the floor rising.

Corporate Takeovers (Macro): This is your structural reset. Breakthroughs optimize the engine; Takeovers buy you a whole new factory.

**Q9: Takeovers without the "punishing reset" feel.**
Instead of wiping the skill tree, Spin it off. When you initiate a Corporate Takeover, that specific skill tree becomes an autonomous subsidiary. You lose direct access to its mini-games and granular upgrades, but it now pays a massive, permanent baseline dividend. This frees up your limited "Active Processing Slots" to start a brand new, higher-tier corporate tree. You aren't losing progress; you're graduating from micro-management to macro-economics.

### Topic D: Mini-Game Architecture

**Q10: Consolidating into 5-6 families.**
Absolutely consolidate. Trying to build 30 distinct, fun mini-games is a development trap. Build 5 robust, highly polished mechanic families:

Tracing/Clearing (Landscaping, Cleaning)

Sorting/Tetris (Logistics, Warehousing)

Rhythm/Timing (Day Trading, Data Mining)

Pathing/Routing (Transport, Hacking)

Placement/Defense (Network Security)

Reskinning and adding modifiers to these 5 families will give you 30+ unique feeling drones without drowning your engineering timeline.

**Q11: Mini-games vs. Idle Output.**
Performance must impact idle, but it needs a hard cap. If you don't cap it, macro-users break the game. If it doesn't affect idle, players will ignore it.

Solution: The mini-game charges a Capacitor. Playing a drone's mini-game perfectly sequences a tight feedback loop (similar to a cherry-to-battery engine in deeper games) that doubles that drone's idle production for the next 4 hours. Once the capacitor is full, further active play yields nothing. This encourages the player to log in, play the mini-game well once to "spin up the plates," and then walk away.

### Topic E: Content Systems

**Q12: Internal vs. External Teases.**
Split them. This perfectly mirrors the AI's internal state versus its external impact.

Corrupted Memory Fragments (Internal): These tease the Protocols (Landscaping, Combat, Transport). They are inward-looking, glitchy, and lore-heavy. "Who was I?"

Heard On The Network (External): These tease the Specialist Drones. They are outward-looking, grounded, and reactive. A news snippet reads: "Local municipal services baffled by rogue street sweepers moving at 40mph." This makes the player feel the real-world impact of their expanding idle empire.
